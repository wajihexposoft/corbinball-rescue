UPDATED ARTICLE: 5 Easy Steps to Guard Against Computer Viruses I continue to get numerous emails with worm attachments. The latest has been several copies of the goner worm. The fundamental rule to avoid these viruses and worms is never open up file attachments ending with the following attachments: .exe .vbs .bat .scr .pif The article has been updated to cover the new virus extensions and can be found at: http://www.corbinball.com/articles/art-virus.htm NEW ARTICLE: How To Spot Computer Virus Hoaxes Virus hoaxes are alive and well too, and in fact, are more prevalent than real viruses. I have recently received many virus hoax messages urging me to delete an important Windows file: SULFNBK.EXE saying that this file was a virus. These messages followed a typical pattern that should ring warning bells. In general, any message that asks you to copy and send to everyone in your address book should be treated with suspicion.
