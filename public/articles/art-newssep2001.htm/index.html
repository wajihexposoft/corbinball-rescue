Check out her Tourism Internet Marketing Boot Camp: 27 & 28 September, 2001, Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada New Article: Death of the Meeting Industry Dot-Com? Key Questions to Ask Before Buy. Boom to bust. Pets.com, ToySmart, Rx.com, Music.com, Wine.com, Zap.com, CDWorld, eToys, MusicMaker.com, Garden.com and at least 600 other Internet companies have unplugged in the past 18 months. Companies formed without much thought to a business model sprouted like daisies and now are gone with the first frost of an economic downturn. Venture capital funding has dried up, and many online companies are worth pennies to the dollar or less. How will this affect us in the meetings industry? What about the hundreds of online technology providers and Internet companies servicing meeting planners? There have already been layoffs and downsizing for many of the companies and even failures (i.e. MeetHead.com). Are most of these companies doomed? What questions can a planner ask to get a sense of the health of these companies? What can planners do to protect their organization when dealing with dot-coms? What's the worst-case scenario if a company goes under? NEW FAVORITES Teleportec www.teleportec.com This company cleverly has figured out a way to project a human presenter behind a lectern (and even on a stage) to give the appearance that the person is standing there in real life! (Think of the Star Wars scene where R2D2 projected the image of Princess Lea � but in full size.) This is teleconferencing on steroids! With a two-way video feed, the presenter can interact with the audience and respond to them as if he/she were on the platform. PowerPoint slides and other images can be advanced as if the person was there. Using ISDN or T1 lines and, ultimately, Internet2 technology, this has the potential to revolutionize teleconferences and has enormous potential for presenters and educators as well. DigiMask www.digimask.com This one will give you the creeps! This site allows you to submit two �mug-shot� digital photos of yourself (a profile and a front shot).
